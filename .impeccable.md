# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Casual NBA fans and social sports fans — people who watch games with friends and want a fun social layer around the experience. They're on their phones during games, in group chats, at watch parties. The app is where they log games, share takes, compete on rankings, and now play party games (Codenames) together. Context is relaxed but engaged — couch, bar, group hangout.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, Fun, Premium** — high-energy but polished. Think slick sports broadcast meets gaming lobby. The app should feel like the coolest sports app in your friend group — not corporate, not sterile, but confident and well-crafted.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-only. Deep black backgrounds (`#0a0a0a`) with warm gold accent (`#c9a84c`). AMOLED-optimized.
- **Typography**: Space Grotesk — geometric, modern, confident. Use weight contrast (400 vs 700) for hierarchy.
- **Colors**: Gold for primary actions/CTAs, team colors for contextual data, red (`#e63946`) for alerts/danger, muted gray (`#6b7280`) for secondary text.
- **Surfaces**: Three-tier depth — background → surface (`#1a1a1a`) → elevated. Subtle borders (`#2a2a2a`), transparent overlays (15-30% opacity) for color accents.
- **Icons**: Lucide icon set, consistent stroke width.
- **References**: Discord and gaming apps — social, real-time, community-driven. Cards with presence indicators, live state, interactive elements.
- **Anti-references**: No generic Bootstrap/Material design. No sparse/sterile minimalism. Should always feel alive with personality and energy.

### Design Principles

1. **Arena energy** — The interface should feel like being at the game. Dark, immersive, with pops of team color and gold highlights. Dramatic but not heavy.

2. **Thumb-first** — Every interaction is designed for one-handed mobile use. Large tap targets, bottom-anchored actions, swipe-friendly. No tiny buttons or desktop-first layouts.

3. **Show, don't decorate** — Every visual element earns its place. Team logos, color-coded cards, and live indicators are information, not decoration. Avoid ornamental borders, gradients, or effects that don't communicate meaning.

4. **Responsive feedback** — Every tap should feel alive. Spring animations on cards, haptic feedback on actions, subtle scale/opacity transitions. The app should feel snappy and physical.

5. **Friend group vibes** — The tone is confident but never stuffy. Copy is casual ("Your Turn", not "It is currently your turn to play"). UI feels like something you'd show off to friends, not present in a boardroom.
